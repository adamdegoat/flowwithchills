Iylia, your guide

Meet your guide

He used to build bodies. Now he rebuilds heads.

Iylia ran a gym for years. He watched people chase a stronger body while their sleep, their focus and their calm quietly fell apart. Strong on the outside, running on empty underneath. He figured out the work he cared about was never really about muscle.

So he swapped the gym floor for forest trails, waterfalls and quiet coast. Someone who understands training, recovery and how the body holds stress, walking your team back to steady, one route at a time.

  • Ex gym owner and coach
  • Breathwork and movement

A day on retreat

One route, four legs, zero notifications.

  1. Morning

    Arrive and unplug

    Phones go in the bag. We open with slow movement and breathwork to shake off the week and land everyone in the same place.

  2. Late morning

    Move through it

    A guided trail, a waterfall, a stretch of coast. Paced for every fitness level, built to get people moving together, not competing.

  3. Afternoon

    Rest and reset

    Stillness, a shared meal, and simple prompts that pull the team back to being people, not just colleagues.

  4. To finish

    Carry it back

    Everyone leaves with a small, honest set of habits to protect their energy long after the retreat ends.

Why nature works

The calm isn't a nice-to-have. It's the whole point.

a

The nervous system stands down

Green space lowers the stress response. Slower pulse, clearer head, a body that stops bracing.

b

Attention refills

Away from the ping of notifications, focus and ideas come back. People return sharper, not just softer.

c

People drop the armour

Something honest happens on a trail that never happens in a meeting room. Trust goes up on its own.

d

It actually sticks

A retreat is a reset, not a plaster. People leave with habits they can keep on a normal Tuesday.
